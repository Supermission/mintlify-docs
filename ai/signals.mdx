# AI Signals

A signal fires when our AI thinks the market is wrong about something.

We run analysis on every active market. When the AI's estimated probability diverges from the current market price, that gap becomes a signal. Bigger gap, stronger signal.

## Three types

**Sentiment.** The core signal. Four agents analyze a market using news and social data, debate each other, and produce a probability. If it's meaningfully different from the market price, you see it here.

**Momentum.** Fires when the price is already moving in the direction the AI predicted. Confirmation that the market is catching up to what the AI saw earlier.

**Spot Price.** Crypto-specific. For markets like "Will Bitcoin be above $100K by Friday," this compares the current spot price to the market's threshold and gives you a proximity signal.

## How they're made

Every 15 minutes, the system pulls news from 23 RSS feeds plus Reddit and Bluesky. It matches articles to markets using semantic similarity. Relevant matches go through the four-agent system (Bull, Bear, Contrarian, Quant), which produces a probability and reasoning.

Confidence scores are agreement-weighted. If all four agents agree, confidence is high. If they're split, it caps lower regardless of what any single agent says.

Filter by category (Crypto, Politics, Sports), signal type, minimum confidence, or minimum volume. Sort by confidence, edge size, or ending soonest.
