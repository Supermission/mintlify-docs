# Whale Intelligence

Open any market's detail panel. Scroll down. You'll see who holds the most shares and which side they're on.

## What it shows

A sentiment bar at the top splits holders into YES and NO camps. Below that, a list of the top holders with their position sizes.

Each row has a background tint proportional to the holder's position. The biggest whale has a full-width tint. Smaller holders barely show. You can read the power distribution at a glance without looking at numbers.

If holders with profile pictures on Polymarket are in the list, you'll see their avatars.

## Smart money indicator

When more than 55% of whale capital leans one direction, a callout appears: "Smart money leans YES" or "Smart money leans NO."

This isn't a trading signal on its own. Big wallets are wrong plenty of times. But when the AI signals and the whales agree, that's worth paying attention to.

## Stats

Three numbers above the holder list. Total whales tracked. Total shares held. And how much of the position the top holder controls (concentration risk).

If one wallet holds 30%+ of all shares, the concentration number turns amber. That's a warning, not necessarily bad, just something to factor in.
