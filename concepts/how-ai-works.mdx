# How our AI finds opportunities

Supermission doesn't use a single AI model to predict market outcomes. That would be too easy to fool and too narrow in perspective.

Instead, we run a **structured debate** between four AI agents — each with a completely different job. They argue. A judge synthesizes their best arguments. And you see everything: the reasoning, the disagreements, the confidence level.

This is the engine behind [Signals](/ai/signals), [War Room](/ai/war-room), and [Alpha Scanner](/ai/alpha-scanner).

## The four agents

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Bull Agent" icon="arrow-up">
    **Job:** Find every reason the market should be higher.

    Looks for catalysts, positive sentiment shifts, underpriced probabilities, momentum confirmation, and institutional interest.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Bear Agent" icon="arrow-down">
    **Job:** Find every reason it could go wrong.

    Looks for risk factors, negative data, overconfidence in consensus, structural headwinds, and historical patterns that suggest lower probability.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Contrarian Agent" icon="rotate">
    **Job:** Ask "what if everyone is wrong?"

    Looks for crowded trades, false consensus, scenarios the market isn't pricing, regime changes, and black swan potential.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Quant Agent" icon="chart-mixed">
    **Job:** Pure data, no narratives.

    Looks at historical base rates, volume patterns, orderbook dynamics, time-to-resolution analysis, price velocity, and liquidity depth.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How they work together

<Steps>
  <Step title="Data collection">
    Every agent gets the same starting information: current market price, trading volume, orderbook depth, resolution date, and category. Then each agent independently pulls from their own sources — news, social media sentiment, historical patterns, and price data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Independent analysis">
    Each agent works alone. They don't see each other's work. Each produces a probability estimate (0-100%), a confidence level, key evidence supporting their view, and the biggest doubt that could prove them wrong.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Judge synthesis">
    A judge agent reviews all four analyses and produces a final verdict: a synthesized probability, confidence score, the single biggest risk, and how many agents agree with each other.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Signal fires">
    If the AI's probability diverges meaningfully from the market price — that's a signal. You see it in your feed with the full reasoning chain attached, so you can decide for yourself.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding confidence scores

| Score | What it means | Agent Agreement |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| **80-100** | High conviction — the agents largely agree and have strong evidence | 3 or 4 out of 4 aligned |
| **60-79** | Moderate conviction — meaningful debate between agents | 2 out of 4 aligned |
| **40-59** | Low conviction — proceed with caution | Agents are split |

## Conviction levels

The War Room classifies every analysis into one of four levels:

- **Extreme** — All 4 agents agree with high confidence. You'll get an alert with a sound notification.
- **High** — 3 out of 4 agents agree. Strong directional signal.
- **Moderate** — 2 out of 4 agree. Evidence on both sides.
- **Contested** — Agents are split evenly. The market is genuinely uncertain.

## How often markets get analyzed

High-profile markets (trending or high-volume) get the full 4-agent debate frequently. Lower-volume markets get lighter analysis less often. When breaking news hits a market, it gets re-analyzed immediately regardless of schedule.

## Sports markets work differently

Traditional news and sentiment analysis doesn't work well for sports — outcomes depend on live performance, not information edges. For sports markets, we use specialized price-movement detection that watches for:

- **Sharp moves** — sudden price swings that suggest informed betting
- **Volume spikes** — unusual activity that signals something is happening
- **Spread compression** — when the gap between buy and sell prices narrows sharply
- **Late action** — significant price movement close to game time

<Info>
  The AI system improves over time. After each market resolves, we score the prediction and feed the result back into the system. Better calibration, better signals.
</Info>

<Warning>
  AI predictions are not financial advice. The multi-agent system improves signal quality but cannot predict the future. Markets are adversarial — always understand the reasoning chain before acting on a signal.
</Warning>
