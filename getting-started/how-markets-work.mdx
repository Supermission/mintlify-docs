# How prediction markets work

A prediction market asks a yes-or-no question. "Will the Fed cut rates in March?" "Will Bitcoin hit $100K by Friday?"

You buy shares in the outcome you think is correct.

**YES shares** pay $1 each if the answer turns out to be yes. $0 if no.
**NO shares** pay $1 each if the answer turns out to be no. $0 if yes.

The current price tells you what the market thinks the probability is. If YES is trading at 65 cents, the market thinks there's roughly a 65% chance.

## Making money

Buy YES at 30 cents. If you're right, each share pays $1. That's 70 cents profit per share. If you're wrong, you lose your 30 cents.

The math is simple. Your profit is $1 minus what you paid. Your risk is what you paid.

## Resolution

Markets resolve based on real-world outcomes. An oracle (UMA) determines the result. Once resolved, winning shares become redeemable for $1 each. Go to your Portfolio, hit Redeem.

## Selling early

You don't have to wait for resolution. If the price moves in your favor, you can sell your shares to someone else at the current market price. Take profit early if you want.
