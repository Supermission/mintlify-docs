# Managing risk

Prediction markets are gambling with a probability wrapper. Don't let the charts and percentages trick you into thinking you can't lose.

## Position sizing

Don't put more than 5-10% of your total balance on any single market. If you have $100, max bet is $10. Sounds conservative. That's the point.

## Diversify

Spread across categories. If all your positions are on political markets and something unexpected happens, everything moves against you at once.

## Time matters

Markets ending soon are more volatile. A 60% market with 2 days left moves differently than a 60% market with 6 months left. Short-dated markets can swing 20 points in hours.

## The AI is not magic

Our signals have a good track record but they're not always right. A 90% confidence signal still loses 1 in 10 times. Size accordingly.

## Limit orders

Use limit orders when you can. You set your price and wait. No slippage, no paying more than you wanted. Market orders fill instantly but at whatever the current best price is.

<Warning>
  There is no stop-loss feature yet. If you need to limit downside, set a mental stop and check your positions regularly.
</Warning>
