# Browsing markets

The Browse tab is your starting point. Every active market on Polymarket is displayed as a card in a scrollable grid. Here's what each element means and how to use it.

## What's on a market card

Each card gives you a quick snapshot of a market so you can decide whether to dig deeper.

### Header

| Element | What it tells you |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Market icon** | The category — politics, crypto, sports, etc. |
| **Question** | The core question being traded on. Truncated on the card; full text in the detail panel. |
| **AI Pick badge** | Shows when our AI has a view. **Green** = AI favors Yes, **Red** = AI favors No, **Gray** = neutral. |
| **Asset tag** | If the market tracks a specific asset (like BTC or ETH), you'll see a small badge. |
| **Timeframe tag** | Shows the market's timeframe category, if applicable. |

### Key stats

| Element | What it tells you |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Category badges** | One or more labels like Politics, Crypto, or Sports. |
| **Trending tag** | An orange badge that appears when the market is seeing unusual activity. |
| **Volume** | Total USDC ever traded. Higher volume means better prices and easier fills. |
| **24h Volume** | USDC traded in the last 24 hours. Tells you how active the market is right now. |
| **Countdown** | Time until the market resolves. **Red** = less than 1 hour, **Amber** = less than 24 hours, **Gray** = more than 24 hours. Resolved markets show "Ended". |

### Prices and actions

| Element | What it tells you |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Probability bars** | Green and red bars showing the Yes and No percentages. Wider bar = higher probability. |
| **Yes / No buttons** | Show the current price in cents. Click either one to start trading. |
| **Bookmark icon** | Save the market to your [Watchlist](/trading/watchlist). Filled icon = already saved. |

### Visual cues

The card's left border tells you the AI's stance at a glance:
- **Green border** — AI favors Yes
- **Red border** — AI favors No
- **No colored border** — No AI signal

When you're actively trading from a card, it highlights with an amber border so you can keep track of where you are.

## Trading directly from a card

You don't need to open the detail panel to place a trade. You can do it right from the card.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick a side">
    Click **Yes** or **No** on any card. An amount picker slides in below the card. Only one card can be in trading mode at a time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your amount">
    Use the quick presets — **$1, $5, $10, $25, $50, $100** — or type a custom amount. You'll instantly see:
    - How many shares you'll get
    - Your average price per share
    - Your potential payout if you're right ($1 per share)
    - Your potential profit (payout minus cost)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    A summary panel appears. Review the details and click **Confirm** to execute. Your order fills instantly at the best available prices.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You're in">
    Your new position shows up immediately in the Portfolio tab. No waiting around for confirmations.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Grid layouts

You can switch between two layouts using the toggle in the sidebar:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Normal View (Grid)">
    Every market gets its own card in a flat grid. One column on mobile, two on tablet, three on desktop. Best for scanning broadly across all markets.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Stacked View (Events)">
    Related markets are grouped under their parent event. For example, an election event with 10+ sub-markets collapses into a single card showing the event title and market count. Click to expand and see all sub-markets. Great for comparing related outcomes. See [Stacked View](/markets/stacked-view) for details.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Scrolling and loading

Markets load in batches of 50 with infinite scroll. As you approach the bottom, the next batch loads automatically. No buttons to click — just keep scrolling.

<Tip>
  The best workflow: filter by category, sort by volume or trending, scan the AI Pick badges for strong signals, then open the detail panel on anything interesting. Always check the orderbook depth, price chart, and AI War Room analysis before placing larger trades.
</Tip>
