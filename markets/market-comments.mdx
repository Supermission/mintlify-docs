# Market comments

Every market has a comments section where traders share their analysis, debate outcomes, and flag new information. It's one of the fastest ways to get a pulse on what the community thinks.

## Where to find them

Comments appear inside the market detail panel, below the orderbook and price chart. Open any market card and scroll down to see the discussion.

## What you'll see

Each comment shows:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Author** | The trader's username or a shortened version of their wallet address |
| **Timestamp** | When the comment was posted, shown as relative time ("2 hours ago", "3 days ago") |
| **Comment text** | The trader's message |
| **Likes** | How many other traders found the comment useful |
| **Replies** | Threaded replies nested under the parent comment |

Comments are sorted so the most relevant discussion surfaces first. Reply threads are indented so you can follow conversations easily.

## How to use comments effectively

### Gauge community sentiment

Before placing a trade, skim the comments to see which way the crowd is leaning. If everyone is bullish, consider whether that consensus is already priced in — or if there's a contrarian opportunity.

### Find information you missed

Other traders often spot news articles, data points, or developments that you might not have seen. Comments can surface information faster than waiting for the AI signals to update.

### Understand the bear case

Look for the most-liked comments arguing against the consensus. These often highlight risks and edge cases that are easy to overlook when you're excited about a trade.

### Track sentiment shifts

Check comments at different times. If the tone shifts from bullish to cautious (or vice versa), it could signal a price move before it shows up in the chart.

## Keep in mind

Comments are written by other traders and are not moderated for accuracy. People have biases, positions, and sometimes bad information. A few ground rules:

- **Don't trade based on a single comment.** Use comments as one input alongside the AI analysis, orderbook data, and your own research.
- **Watch for position bias.** Traders holding large Yes positions have an incentive to post bullish comments. The same goes for No holders.
- **Verify claims independently.** If someone cites a news article or data point, check the source yourself before acting on it.

<Info>
  Comments are community-generated and unmoderated. They may contain opinions, speculation, or inaccurate information. Always do your own research before trading.
</Info>

<Tip>
  The most valuable comments are often the ones that disagree with the current price. If a market is at 80% Yes and someone makes a well-reasoned case for No, that's worth reading carefully — even if you end up disagreeing.
</Tip>
