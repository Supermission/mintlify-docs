# Market comparison

Sometimes you need to see two or more markets next to each other to understand how they relate. The comparison tool lets you overlay up to 4 markets on a single screen.

## How to get there

Open the comparison page from the main navigation. You can also add markets to a comparison directly from the market detail panel.

## Adding markets

Use the search bar at the top of the comparison page to find markets. Type a keyword, pick from the results, and the market gets added to your comparison. You can add up to 4 markets at a time. Markets you've already added won't appear in the search results again.

## What you'll see

### Side-by-side stats

For each market in your comparison, you get:
- The full market question
- Current Yes and No prices
- Volume, liquidity, and 24h price change
- Resolution date
- AI signal, if one exists

### Synchronized price charts

All selected markets appear on the same chart with color-coded lines. This is where comparison gets powerful:

- Every market shares the same timeframe so movements are easy to compare
- Hover over the chart and all markets show their price at that exact moment
- Switch between timeframes: **6H, 1D, 1W, 1M, ALL**
- Spot when markets move together — or when they diverge

## When comparison is useful

| Scenario | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Sanity-checking related outcomes** | "BTC above 100K by June" vs "BTC above 80K by June" — the 100K market should never be priced higher than the 80K one. If it is, something is off. |
| **Comparing angles on the same event** | "Democrats win Senate" vs "Democrats win popular vote" — should a popular vote win translate to a Senate win? |
| **Cross-category correlation** | A crypto price market vs a regulatory policy market — does one predict the other? |
| **Finding arbitrage** | Two markets asking essentially the same question with different prices. |

## Tips for effective comparison

- **Start with Stacked View.** Find an event group, expand it, and compare the sub-markets that seem mispriced relative to each other.
- **Use the chart timeframes.** Two markets might look correlated on a 1-week view but diverge on a 1-month view. Check multiple timeframes.
- **Watch for lagging markets.** When one market moves sharply and a related market hasn't caught up yet, the slower market may be about to move.
- **Combine with AI signals.** If the AI has a strong view on one market but not a related one, the AI's reasoning might apply to both.

<Tip>
  Comparison is most powerful when you pair it with Stacked View. Find an event with several sub-markets, then compare the ones whose prices don't seem consistent with each other.
</Tip>
