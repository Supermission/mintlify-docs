# Market detail panel

Click any market card to open the detail panel on the right side of your screen. This is where you do your real research before committing money. The panel has two main tabs: **Trade** and **AI War Room**.

## Panel header

| Element | What it does |
|---------|--------------|
| **Back button** | Returns to the previous market or closes the panel |
| **Bookmark button** | Save or remove from your watchlist |
| **Close button** | Closes the panel |

## Market info

At the top of the panel you'll find:

- **Question** — the full market question (expandable if it's long)
- **Description** — additional context about what the market covers
- **Tags** — category badges, AI Pick badge, asset tag, timeframe, trending indicators

## Trade tab

This is the default tab. Everything you need to evaluate and place a trade.

### AI signals

If the AI has a view on this market, you'll see it right at the top:

- **Signal type** — Sentiment, Momentum, or Spot Price
- **AI pick** — which outcome the AI favors (Yes or No) with a confidence score
- **Summary** — a brief explanation of why
- **Sources** — links to the news or data the AI used, when available

### Market stats

A quick-reference grid with the numbers that matter:

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Volume** | Total USDC ever traded in this market |
| **24h Volume** | USDC traded in the last 24 hours |
| **Liquidity** | How much you can trade without significantly moving the price |
| **Spread** | The gap between the best buy and sell prices. Tighter is better. |
| **24h Change** | How much the price has moved in the last day |
| **Ends in** | Countdown to when the market resolves |

### Live prices

Large, prominent Yes and No prices in cents. These update in real time. Animated probability bars show the Yes/No split visually.

### Placing orders

You can choose between two order types at the top of the order section:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Market Order">
    Buy at the current best available price. Same flow as trading from the card:
    1. Click **Yes** or **No**
    2. Enter your USDC amount (presets or custom)
    3. See your shares, average price, and potential return
    4. Confirm to execute instantly
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Limit Order">
    Set your own price and wait for the market to come to you:
    1. Pick your outcome (Yes or No)
    2. Enter your price in cents (from $0.01 to $0.99)
    3. Enter your USDC amount or share count — the other calculates automatically
    4. Click **Place Limit Order**
    5. Your order sits on the orderbook until it gets filled or you cancel it

    The form checks that your price is valid and your balance can cover the order.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Orderbook

Two ways to see the current buy and sell interest:

**Ladder view** — a traditional price ladder:
- Green rows on the left show buy orders at each price level
- Red rows on the right show sell orders
- Bar fills show the relative size of orders at each level
- Hover over any level to see cumulative depth

**Depth view** — a visual chart of cumulative order depth:
- Green area shows total buy interest, red shows total sell interest
- The midpoint price sits at the center
- Hover for exact sizes at each level

### Price chart

An interactive chart showing how the market price has moved over time:
- **Timeframes**: 6H, 1D, 1W, 1M, ALL
- **Y-axis**: Probability from 0% to 100%
- Hover for precise prices at any point in time

### Open orders

If you have active limit orders on this market, they appear here with the order details and a cancel button for each one.

### Top holders (Whale Intelligence)

See who's holding the biggest positions:

- **Whale sentiment bar** — shows how large holders are split between Yes and No
- **Key stats**: number of whales, total shares held, how concentrated the top position is
- **Leaderboard**: the top 20 holders with their position sizes and which side they're on

When whales lean more than 55% in one direction, you'll see a "Smart money leans Yes/No" insight.

### Related markets

If this market belongs to an event with multiple sub-markets, you'll see the related ones at the bottom. Click any to navigate without leaving the panel.

### Comments

Community discussion about the market. See what other traders are thinking, debating, and flagging. More details in [Market Comments](/markets/market-comments).

## AI War Room tab

Switch to this tab for the full AI analysis. Five sections give you a complete picture:

1. **Edge Calculator** — compares the AI's confidence with the current market price. Green means the AI thinks the market is underpriced. Red means overpriced.

2. **Conviction Meter** — a visual gauge showing how strongly the AI feels. Below 40% is low conviction, 40–70% is moderate, above 70% is high conviction. At extreme levels (all AI agents agree with high confidence), the meter pulses to grab your attention.

3. **Agent Debate** — four AI agents each share their take: Bull, Bear, Contrarian, and Quant. Each shows their stance (bullish, bearish, or neutral), confidence level, and key reasoning.

4. **Key Doubt** — the Contrarian agent's single biggest risk to the thesis. If you only read one thing, read this.

5. **Accuracy Scoreboard** — how the AI has performed historically on similar markets. Win rate, precision, and calibration data.

See [AI War Room](/ai/war-room) for the full breakdown.

<Tip>
  The ideal workflow: scan Browse for AI Pick badges, open the detail panel, check the Edge Calculator and Conviction Meter, read the Agent Debate for reasoning, review the Key Doubt for risk, look at whale positions, check orderbook liquidity, then place your trade.
</Tip>
