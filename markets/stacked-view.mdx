# Stacked view

Some events have one market. Others have a dozen. A major election might have markets on the overall winner, the popular vote, individual swing states, Senate control, and more — all related, all tradeable separately.

Stacked View groups these related markets under a single expandable card so you can see every angle at once.

## How to turn it on

In the sidebar, toggle the view mode from **Normal** to **Stacked**. The grid reorganizes instantly. Toggle back anytime to return to the flat layout.

## What you'll see

<Steps>
  <Step title="Events replace individual cards">
    When multiple markets belong to the same event, they collapse into a single event card. The card shows the event title, how many sub-markets are inside, the combined trading volume, and a price preview from the most active sub-market.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click to expand">
    Click any event card to open it up. All sub-markets appear in a list with their own prices, volumes, countdowns, and trade buttons. Click any sub-market to open its full detail panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Single-market events stay as cards">
    If an event only has one market, it looks and works exactly like a normal card. No extra clicks needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why it matters

Stacked View isn't just a cosmetic change. It gives you a real analytical edge.

### Spot mispricings between related markets

When you can see "Democrats win Senate" at 60% next to "Democrats win popular vote" at 45%, you can ask whether those numbers make sense together. If they don't, one of those markets might be mispriced — and that's an opportunity.

### Build correlated positions

Want to bet on multiple outcomes within the same event? Stacked View lets you see all your options in one place so you can build a position across related markets without hunting for each one individually.

### Track complex events

Major events like elections, Fed meetings, or big crypto catalysts often spawn many related markets. Stacked View keeps them organized so you don't miss a sub-market buried in the grid.

## When to use each view

| View | Best for |
|------|----------|
| **Normal** | Scanning broadly, discovering new markets across categories, quick trades |
| **Stacked** | Researching a specific event, comparing related outcomes, building multi-market positions, finding mispricings |

## Scrolling works the same

The grid still loads in batches as you scroll. Whether you're in Normal or Stacked View, more markets appear automatically as you reach the bottom.

<Tip>
  Correlated markets are where experienced traders find edge. If two outcomes should be linked but their prices disagree, one of them is likely wrong. Stacked View makes these comparisons effortless.
</Tip>
