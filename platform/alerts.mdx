# Never miss a move

Price alerts are tied to your [Watchlist](/trading/watchlist). When a market you're watching moves more than 5% in either direction, you get notified instantly. No polling dashboards. No refreshing tabs.

## How alerts work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Watchlist a market">
    Bookmark any market by clicking the bookmark icon on its card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for movement">
    Supermission monitors prices on all your watchlisted markets in real-time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="5% threshold triggers">
    When a watchlisted market's price moves more than 5% from your bookmark price (or the last alert), a notification fires.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notification appears">
    A notification card slides in from the top-right of the screen. It shows the market name, direction of movement, and the new price.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Alert sounds

Different sounds for different directions — so you know what happened before you look:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Price Up" icon="arrow-up">
    Distinct ascending tone. The market moved in favor of YES holders.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Price Down" icon="arrow-down">
    Different descending tone. The market moved in favor of NO holders.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Interacting with alerts

- **Click** the notification to jump directly to the market's detail page
- **Swipe** (or dismiss) to clear the notification
- Alerts auto-dismiss after a few seconds if you don't interact

<Info>
  The 5% threshold is calculated from the price at the time you bookmarked the market, or from the price at the last alert — whichever is more recent. This prevents alert fatigue from markets that oscillate around the same level.
</Info>

## Why 5%?

Small moves are noise. A market ticking from 52% to 53% isn't actionable. But a jump from 52% to 58%? Something happened. News broke. A whale entered. The consensus shifted.

5% is the sweet spot between "I want to know" and "stop bothering me."

<Warning>
  Alerts require the Supermission tab to be open (or the PWA installed). We don't send push notifications to your phone's lock screen — yet.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Pair alerts with limit orders. Watchlist a market, wait for the alert that confirms your thesis, then execute. Reactive trading beats predictive trading most of the time.
</Tip>
