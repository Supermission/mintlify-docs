# See the full picture

Some events have one market. Others have ten. The 2024 election had markets on winner, popular vote, swing states, Senate control, and more — all related, all tradeable separately.

Stacked View groups these sub-markets under a single expandable card so you can see all the angles at once.

## How to switch

In the sidebar, toggle between two view modes:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Normal View">
    Flat grid. Every market is its own card. Simple, scannable, no hierarchy. This is the default.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Stacked View">
    Markets grouped by event. Events with 2 or more sub-markets collapse into a single card showing the event title and a count of available markets.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Toggle to Stacked">
    Hit the Stacked View toggle in the sidebar. The market grid reorganizes instantly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse events">
    Each event card shows the event name, number of sub-markets, and aggregate volume. The highest-volume sub-market's price is previewed on the card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expand to explore">
    Click an event card to expand it. All sub-markets appear as a nested list with individual prices, volumes, and trade buttons.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trade any sub-market">
    Click any sub-market within the expanded card to open its full detail page — War Room, whale data, order panel, everything.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Markets that don't belong to a multi-market event appear as normal cards in both views. Stacked View only groups markets that share a parent event.
</Info>

## When to use which

- **Normal View** — browsing broadly, scanning prices, looking for new opportunities
- **Stacked View** — researching a specific event, comparing related markets, building correlated positions

<Tip>
  Correlated markets are where smart traders find mispricings. If the "Democrats win Senate" market is at 60% but "Democrats win popular vote" is at 45%, something might be off. Stacked View makes these comparisons effortless.
</Tip>
