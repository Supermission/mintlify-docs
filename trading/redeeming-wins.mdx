# Collect your winnings

When a prediction market resolves, the outcome is final. If you held the winning side, each share is now worth exactly **$1.00**. Redeeming converts those shares into USDC in your wallet.

## How redemption works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Market resolves">
    The oracle determines the outcome. Your position card updates with a resolution badge — green for wins, red for losses.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Redeem button appears">
    On winning positions, a **Redeem Winnings** button replaces the sell button. The card shows exactly how many shares you hold and the USDC value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click to redeem">
    One click. Your winning shares are converted and USDC is released to your wallet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Funds arrive">
    Your USDC lands in your main wallet balance automatically. No manual steps required. Funds typically appear within a few minutes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The math

It's straightforward:

| Bought | Price Paid | Shares | If You Win |
|--------|-----------|--------|------------|
| YES | $0.35/share | 100 | 100 × $1.00 = **$100** |
| YES | $0.72/share | 50 | 50 × $1.00 = **$50** |
| NO | $0.20/share | 200 | 200 × $1.00 = **$200** |

Your profit = redemption value minus what you paid. That's it.

<Info>
  Losing shares are worth $0.00. They just disappear from your portfolio after resolution. No action needed on your end.
</Info>

## What if I don't redeem?

<Accordion title="Do winnings expire?">
  No. Winning shares sit there until you redeem them. There's no deadline. But there's also no reason to wait — that's capital sitting idle.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I redeem partial shares?">
  Yes, but why would you? Redemption is free (no gas fees). Just redeem everything.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Smart traders redeem immediately and re-deploy. Compound the wins. The moment a market resolves, that capital should be working on your next trade — not sitting in a resolved position.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Redemption requires the market to be fully resolved. Some markets have a dispute period. If the outcome is being challenged, the redeem button won't appear until the dispute resolves.
</Warning>
